Finca El Tomillo

Gualtallary

Parcels 1 and 5 of the Finca El Tomillo are located in the middle part of Gualtallary in the Uco valley… a place that is set to become the most iconic spot in Mendoza giving the finest wines. The finca stands on an alluvial fan built up by streams from the Las Tunas River, about 24 km from the river mouth in the valley, at an altitude of 1210 m. The vines were planted in 2004 in a vertical espalier with the rows orientated North-South. The soil has an initial 60 cm deep sandy stratum that rests on rounded clastic rocks that are covered by up to 90% of calcium carbonate.

Finca Las Mercedes

Cafayate · Salta

Parcel 19 of Finca Las Mercedes is located in the Calchaquí Valleys, in the district of Cafayate in Salta. The valley is flanked by the Aconquija Mountains to the east and the Quilmes high hills to the west, this closed valley gives it fewer sunshine hours. The Calchaquí and Santa María Rivers coalesce in this area, creating a unique microclimate. The parcel is located on the distal area of a Yacochuya River alluvial fan. These old vines plated in 1960 at an altitude of 1632m are trellised with a pergola system oriented North-South. The soil is deep and sandy with a textural composition characterized by the presence of 2% clay, 89% sand and 9% silt. These soils originated from metamorphic and igneous granite-type rocks.

Finca Los Chañares

Catamarca

Parcel 73 of Finca Las Chanares is located in the Southern Calchaquí Valleys at an altitude of 1,950m in the district of Santa María, in the province of Catamarca. Similar to the Finca Las Mercedes up north, it is a closed valley with limited hours of sunshine, however it’s soil is much stonier and has a higher lime content. The valley is flanked by the Aconquija Mountains to the east and the Quilmes high hills to the west. The estate stands on the slopes of the Quilmes high hills, in the apex and mid-section of two alluvial fans formed by the Agua del Sapo stream. The vines were planted in 2000, trellised in a vertical espalier system orientated North-South. The soil is sandy with an abundant presence of clasts, which in places make up 60% of the soil volume. The textural composition is characterized by the presence of 96% sand and 4% silt. The origin of the soils come from igneous and sedimentary rocks. The clasts are flat and sub-angular that measure 5-15 cm in diameter.

Finca La Yesca

San Juan

Parcel 13 of the Finca La Yesca is located in Pedernal at an altitude of 1400m which is in the district of San Carlos, the southernmost point of San Juan province, it is an area of extremes with very rocky soils and high temperatures. The Finca stands on the alluvial fan of Las Cortaderas River, that runs along the eastern foothills of the Osamentas Range, in the Paramillos area of the Tontal Mountains. The vines were planted in 2009, trellised on a vertical espalier system and oriented NorthWest-SouthEast, following the angle formed by the alluvial fan. The sandy loam soil constits of 3% clay, 78% sand and 19% silt. The first stratum, at a depth of 50 cm, displays 25% clast content, while the second stratum, starting at a depth of 50 cm and finishing at 150 cm, presents 50% rock content. The clasts are flat, with angular limestone-like edges.
